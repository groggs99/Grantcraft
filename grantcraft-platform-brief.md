# GrantCraft Platform Brief

## Vision

GrantCraft is more than a grant application tool — it's the platform where Irish community organisations apply for funding, manage their projects, demonstrate impact, and connect with each other. Every step of the journey from "we have an idea" to "here's what we delivered" happens in one place, made easier and more enjoyable by AI and community.

## The Problem Today

Community organisations in Ireland face a fragmented, isolating, and tedious funding journey:

- **Discovery is overwhelming.** Hundreds of schemes across departments, agencies, local authorities, and foundations. No single place to find what's relevant.
- **Applications are painful.** Each funder has different forms, requirements, and language. Groups spend weeks writing applications or pay €500–€2,000 for a grant consultant.
- **There's no support network.** Groups figure everything out alone. A GAA club in Donegal doesn't know what worked for a GAA club in Wexford.
- **Post-award management is chaos.** Spreadsheets, email threads, paper receipts. Reporting deadlines get missed. Budget tracking is manual.
- **Impact is invisible.** Communities rarely see what their local groups applied for, won, or delivered. There's no public accountability and no celebration of what's been achieved.
- **Institutional memory is lost.** When a committee chair steps down, the knowledge of how to apply, what worked, and what funders expect walks out the door with them.

## The Platform

### Stage 1 — Organisation Profile

The organisation creates a profile once. GrantCraft captures:

- Legal structure (CLG, CIC, unincorporated, sports club)
- Location (county, town/village)
- Activity areas (sport, environment, heritage, youth, social enterprise, etc.)
- Existing assets and facilities
- Committee structure and key contacts
- Past funding history (auto-populated over time as they use the platform)
- Community context (population, demographics, other local groups)

This profile is reused across every application. The more they use GrantCraft, the richer and more effective it becomes.

### Stage 2 — Idea Development

Before matching to a grant, the organisation describes what they want to do. GrantCraft helps them shape a vague idea ("we want to do something with our pitch") into a fundable project concept with:

- Clear objectives
- Target beneficiaries
- Estimated costs
- Measurable outcomes
- Alignment to funding priorities

AI guides this process conversationally, drawing on what works in successful applications.

### Stage 3 — Grant Matching

GrantCraft matches the project concept and organisation profile against the grant database. Results are ranked by fit and show:

- Scheme name and funder
- Maximum award available
- Eligibility match score
- Key deadlines
- What the application involves (complexity indicator)
- Success tips specific to that scheme

Users can explore multiple matches and choose which to pursue.

### Stage 4 — Application Generation

GrantCraft generates a complete draft application tailored to the specific scheme, including:

- Narrative sections (need, objectives, methodology, outcomes, sustainability)
- Budget breakdown with realistic cost lines (including GrantCraft platform fee as an eligible line item)
- Supporting evidence suggestions
- Letters of support templates
- Scheme-specific language and formatting

The user reviews, edits, and owns the final application. GrantCraft makes the first draft effortless.

### Stage 5 — Project Dashboard (Post-Award)

Once an application is submitted — and especially once it's funded — the platform becomes a project management space. Committee members log in and see:

- **Active projects** with status indicators
- **Budget tracker** — approved budget vs actual spend, with simple expense logging
- **Milestone timeline** — key deliverables and dates from the original application
- **Reporting deadlines** — countdown to interim and final reports with auto-generated templates pre-populated from the application
- **Document store** — receipts, quotes, photos, and evidence in one place
- **Team access** — multiple committee members can view and contribute

This solves the number one post-award problem: groups losing track of what they committed to and scrambling when reports are due.

### Stage 6 — Community & Public Accountability

Two layers of community:

**1. The GrantCraft Network (private)**

A community of verified GrantCraft users — the people actually applying for and managing grants. This is where:

- Organisations share what worked and what didn't
- Someone who got Sports Capital funding answers questions from a first-time applicant
- Committee members in similar organisations connect (men's sheds helping men's sheds, coastal groups helping coastal groups)
- GrantCraft shares tips, deadline reminders, and scheme updates
- The social contract lives — being part of this network is valuable, and honouring your fee agreement is the price of continued access

This community likely lives on Facebook (where Irish community groups already are) with deeper integration inside the platform over time.

**2. The Public Impact View (public)**

A public-facing layer where communities can see what's happening locally:

- "Bettystown received €40k for a coastal walkway — here's the progress"
- A town-by-town or county-by-county view of funded projects
- Progress updates from organisations who choose to share publicly
- A live sense of how much funding GrantCraft has helped unlock across Ireland

This serves multiple purposes: public accountability, community pride, local media stories, and social proof that drives new sign-ups.

### Stage 7 — Town Discovery & Growth Loop

When one organisation in a town uses GrantCraft, the platform identifies other groups in the area and matches them to relevant grants. This generates:

- Public posts: "GrantCraft just helped a Bettystown group apply. Here's what other Bettystown groups could win."
- Direct outreach to local groups via their public Facebook pages
- A town-level funding opportunity page showing what's available locally
- Referrals from existing users ("what other groups are active in your area?")

This creates organic, location-based growth. One user per town seeds the next five.

## The Flywheel

Each cycle strengthens the next:

1. **Organisation joins** → profile created, idea shaped, grant matched
2. **Application submitted** → GrantCraft fee included in budget
3. **Grant awarded** → project dashboard activated, fee paid from grant
4. **Project managed** → milestones tracked, reports generated, evidence collected
5. **Impact demonstrated** → public view updated, community sees results
6. **Track record built** → next application is stronger, faster, and more credible
7. **Other local groups see it** → they join, cycle repeats across the town

The organisation's history on GrantCraft becomes an asset. Past applications, outcomes, budgets, and reports make each subsequent application better. Leaving the platform means losing that history and starting from scratch elsewhere.

## The Social Contract

GrantCraft is free to use. The fee model is:

- A GrantCraft platform fee (2–3% of grant value) is included as a budget line in each application
- If the application succeeds, the organisation pays the fee from the grant funds
- If the application fails, nothing is owed
- Organisations who don't honour the agreement lose access to GrantCraft and the community network
- Enforcement is reputational — these are local, reputation-driven organisations who sit on multiple committees and are visible in their communities

The fee replaces the cost of a grant consultant (typically €500–€2,000 or 5–10%) at a fraction of the price, and costs the organisation nothing out of pocket.

## What Makes This Defensible

- **Network effects**: the community and peer knowledge get more valuable with every user
- **Data moat**: every application, outcome, and report builds a dataset no competitor can replicate
- **Institutional memory**: organisations can't easily leave without losing their history
- **Location-based growth**: town-by-town expansion creates dense local adoption
- **First mover**: no equivalent platform exists in Ireland for community-level grants

## MVP vs Full Vision

**MVP (get into hands fast):**
- Organisation profile (Stage 1)
- Grant matching against the 76-grant database (Stage 3)
- Application generation with budget and fee line (Stage 4)
- Simple application status tracking (submitted / successful / unsuccessful)
- Facebook Group for the community layer

**Next phase:**
- Idea development with AI guidance (Stage 2)
- Project dashboard with budget and milestone tracking (Stage 5)
- Public impact view (Stage 6)
- Town discovery and outreach automation (Stage 7)

**Future:**
- Funder-side tools (funders seeing aggregated demand, application quality trends)
- Integration with funder portals for direct submission
- Historical success rate data by scheme, region, and organisation type
- AI-powered reporting that drafts progress reports from project dashboard data
